Bonzo Lend Loses $9M in Oracle Exploit on Hedera

Hedera-based lending protocol Bonzo Lend lost approximately $9 million after an attacker exploited a flaw in Supra's on-chain oracle verifier to manipulate the price of SAUCE tokens. The attacker deposited just 250 SAUCE — worth only a few dollars — before submitting a price update that inflated the token's value by roughly 12 orders of magnitude. The wallet then drained 6.63 million USDC and 34.5 million wrapped HBAR from the lending pool. Bonzo attributed the incident to a flaw in Supra's oracle verifier, which accepted a manipulated SAUCE price carrying a zeroed signature. Supra acknowledged the issue and deployed a fix.

Pakistan Crypto Regulator Seeks Dialogue After Islamic Ruling Against Crypto

Pakistan Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (PVARA) chairman Bilal bin Saqib has called for continued dialogue on digital assets under Islamic law after meeting prominent scholar Mufti Taqi Usmani, who backed a ruling against purchases made with crypto. The ruling, issued by Jamia Darul Uloom Karachi and signed by Usmani and five other scholars, declared that purchases with crypto including USDT were not permitted under Islamic law. Saqib said different digital-asset categories merit "careful technical assessment alongside rigorous Shariah examination." The exchange highlights tension between Pakistan's push for a regulated crypto market and religious objections affecting a population that is 96% Muslim.

Michael Saylor and Adam Back Slam BIP-110 Ordinals Proposal

Strategy executive chairman Michael Saylor and Blockstream CEO Adam Back have doubled down on their opposition to BIP-110, a proposed temporary fork to limit non-monetary transactions on the Bitcoin network. The proposal, introduced in December 2025, seeks to stop Ordinals inscriptions and arbitrary data from "spamming" the network. While critical of Ordinals activity, Saylor and Back fear a fork could do more harm than good to the network's credibility. "There are 110 things more dangerous to Bitcoin than spam," Saylor said on X, adding that BIP-110 could invalidate ordinary transactions on the network.

Cambridge Study: Ethereum Among Lowest PoS Energy Intensity

A new Cambridge University study placed Ethereum near the lower end of energy intensity among major proof-of-stake blockchains. Cambridge estimated Ethereum consumes about 7.87 GWh annually — using roughly 33 kWh per $1M of market value, the second-lowest behind BNB Chain. Solana used the most electricity at 13.48 GWh per year with 283 kWh per $1M. The study provides one of the most detailed post-merge assessments yet, finding 56.4% of Ethereum's electricity mix came from renewable and nuclear sources.

IMF: Stablecoins Improve FX Access but Amplify Currency Runs

An IMF working paper by economist Brandon Joel Tan found that US dollar stablecoins can improve access to foreign currency in economies with fixed exchange rates, but may amplify currency runs during severe exchange-rate stress. The paper models how stablecoins affect parallel FX markets when official dollar access is rationed. Stablecoins create a visible, high-frequency price for dollar demand — and when official rates diverge from market rates, that price can signal growing scarcity and prompt more people to abandon the local currency simultaneously.
