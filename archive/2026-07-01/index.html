Taiwan's Legislature Passes Nation's First Crypto and Stablecoin Laws

Taiwan's legislative branch has passed the nation's first comprehensive crypto and stablecoin regulations, establishing a licensing framework for all virtual asset service providers. The new law requires stablecoin issuers to obtain approval from both the central bank and the Financial Supervisory Commission, maintain sufficient reserves with a trustee, and undergo regular audits. Violators face three to ten years in prison and fines of up to $6.3 million, bringing Taiwan in line with regulated crypto hubs like Japan, Singapore, and Hong Kong.
