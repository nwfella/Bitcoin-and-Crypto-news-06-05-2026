Bitcoin ETFs Lose Record $4.5B in June Outflows

US-listed spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds posted a record $4.5 billion in net outflows in June, more than three times the $1.25 billion Strategy is authorized to raise through its new Bitcoin monetization program. BlackRock's iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT) accounted for about 79% of June's withdrawals, posting $3.55 billion in net outflows. The record monthly withdrawals pushed US spot Bitcoin ETFs to roughly $5.5 billion in year-to-date net outflows for 2026, reducing cumulative net inflows since the funds launched to about $51.2 billion, according to SoSoValue data. CryptoQuant data shows the funds now hold less Bitcoin than they did at the same time last year, with total holdings falling below 1.25 million BTC.

Crédit Agricole Launches EURXT Euro Stablecoin on Ethereum

Crédit Agricole, Europe's third-largest bank by assets, has launched the EURO eXchange Token (EURXT), a euro-backed stablecoin issued through its asset servicing arm CACEIS. Issued on the Ethereum blockchain with 20.02 million tokens in circulation at launch, EURXT is an electronic money token pegged 1:1 to the euro, initially targeted at institutional investors and corporate clients. The launch adds to growing competition among traditional financial institutions exploring stablecoins and tokenization, as major banks move to bring blockchain-based settlement into mainstream financial markets. CACEIS secured a MiCA crypto-asset service provider license from French regulators in June 2025.

Taiwan's Legislature Passes First Crypto and Stablecoin Laws

Taiwan's legislative branch has passed the nation's first comprehensive crypto and stablecoin regulations, establishing a licensing framework for all virtual asset service providers. The new law requires stablecoin issuers to obtain approval from both the central bank and the Financial Supervisory Commission, maintain sufficient reserves with a trustee, and undergo regular audits. Violators face three to ten years in prison and fines of up to $6.3 million, bringing Taiwan in line with regulated crypto hubs like Japan, Singapore, and Hong Kong.
