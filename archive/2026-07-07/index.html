Coinbase Gets UK License to Expand into Derivatives and Equities

Coinbase announced it secured a United Kingdom investment services license, allowing the platform to expand beyond spot crypto trading into products like equities and derivatives. The authorization will enable institutional and advanced traders to access perpetual futures tied to crypto, equities and commodities, while retail users will gain access to equities trading. FCA research estimates around 7 million UK adults hold crypto assets, with a quarter of non-holders saying clearer regulation would make them more likely to participate. The authorization comes ahead of the UK's new crypto regime set to accept applications in September and take effect in October 2027.

Ctrl Wallet to Shut Down Weeks After Security Exploit — Users Urged to Withdraw

Non-custodial multichain cryptocurrency wallet Ctrl Wallet, formerly XDEFI Wallet, will shut down its services weeks after a June 23 security exploit, telling users on Tuesday to withdraw their assets by August 3, 2026. EU Lawmakers Adopt Digital Assets Policy Stance After MiCA Transition Ends

European Union lawmakers on Tuesday adopted a position paper on digital assets, setting out their formal policy view on how the bloc should approach crypto regulation after MiCA's rollout. The paper calls on the European Commission to assess whether DeFi, crypto lending and borrowing, staking and NFTs need to be brought more clearly into the EU's regulatory perimeter. It also urges consistent MiCA application across member states and warns against national rules that could fragment the bloc's digital asset market. The vote turns the report into Parliament's formal policy position but does not directly amend MiCA or create new legal obligations for crypto firms. MiCA's transitional period ended July 1, requiring crypto-asset service providers to obtain bloc-wide or national authorization to continue operating across the European Union.

US Bitcoin Reserve Hits Snag as Federal Agencies Debate Control

The Trump administration's push to establish a US Strategic Bitcoin Reserve has reportedly hit a roadblock, as the Commerce and Treasury departments are at odds over how the reserve should be structured and which agency should have primary oversight of the holdings. President Trump's March 2025 executive order called for the SBR to be housed inside Treasury, but concerns have emerged over whether Treasury has the legal authority to manage Bitcoin holdings partly because of its volatility. The Commerce Department has emerged as a contender to oversee the reserve, and the Department of Justice is reportedly working with both departments to determine legally available options. The Bitcoin reserve is a key part of Trump's plan to make the US the "crypto capital of the world," positioning Bitcoin as a strategic reserve asset rather than a seized commodity.

Bitcoin Recovers from Strategy's BTC Sale — Funding Rates Hit 9%

Bitcoin has shaken off the initial shock of Strategy's $216 million BTC sale, recovering above $64,000 as traders assess whether the selling pressure has been absorbed. Funding rates in the perpetual futures market have climbed to 9%, suggesting growing bullish conviction among leveraged longs. The recovery demonstrates that institutional selling, when transparent and expected, may have a more muted impact than forced liquidations.

✦ European Parliament Adopts Landmark Digital Assets Policy — DeFi, Staking, NFTs in Focus ✦

The European Parliament's overwhelming approval of its digital assets policy position marks a defining moment for crypto regulation in Europe. The report — "Digital assets – challenges for the competitiveness and integrity of the European Union's financial system" — was adopted with broad cross-party support and represents the Parliament's formal policy stance on digital assets following MiCA's July 1 transition deadline. Crucially, the paper calls on the European Commission to evaluate whether decentralized finance protocols, crypto lending and borrowing platforms, staking services, and non-fungible tokens require additional regulatory clarity under the EU framework. It also warns member states against introducing national rules that could fragment the single market for digital assets. While the vote does not directly amend MiCA or create new legal obligations, it sets the political foundation for the next phase of EU crypto regulation and signals that European policymakers are actively studying the regulatory treatment of DeFi and Web3 technologies. The report arrives as EU regulators ramp up enforcement — Belgium's FSMA this week flagged six unauthorized crypto providers operating without MiCA authorization.
