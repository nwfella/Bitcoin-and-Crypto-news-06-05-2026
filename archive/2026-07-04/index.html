Bollinger Bands Creator Hints BTC 'W' Reversal Could Break the Bears

Bitcoin is completing a "perfectly fractal" reversal pattern that John Bollinger, creator of the Bollinger Bands volatility indicator, hopes could end the bear market. In X posts on Friday, Bollinger eyed a "W"-shaped double bottom on BTC/USD, noting that the current setup aligns neatly with the lower band of the indicator on daily time frames. "Will this 'W' be the one that breaks the trend?" he asked, pointing to small 'w' patterns at the nadirs and a small 'm' at the apex. Bitcoin reclaimed $62,800 on Saturday, extending its recovery from a near two-year low of $58,190 set on June 25. Analysts broadly believe the next macro bottom is still due in Q3 or later, but Bollinger's signal has renewed optimism that the worst may be passing.

Bitcoin Realized P&L Ratio Falls to 43-Month Low — Historically a Bottom Signal

Bitcoin's realized profit and loss ratio has fallen to a 43-month low of -0.35, a figure that signals extreme market-wide loss conditions but has historically coincided with market bottoms, according to CryptoQuant. The metric hasn't fallen this low since December 2022, shortly after FTX's collapse. "Historically the indicator has marked BTC bottoms with extreme precision," CryptoQuant said. In 2015 and 2019, the ratio fell below -0.35 before price rallies followed. Bitwise CIO Matt Hougan said the bottom is "closer than ever," while Swan Bitcoin analyst Adam Livingston recommended buying now at a discount rather than overpaying later.

EU MiCA Transition Ends — Enforcement Challenge Begins

The European Union's crypto industry has entered a new enforcement phase as MiCA's transition period ends. Crypto companies without MiCA authorization can no longer legally serve EU clients and face multimillion-euro fines. Industry executives and lawyers warn that enforcement is unlikely to be uniform across member states, as national regulators differ in resources and priorities. Compliance costs range from $400,000 to $690,000, but penalties start at 5 million euros or 5% of annual turnover.
