✦ StarkWare CEO Suggests Scrapping Bitcoin's 21M Cap for 4% Annual Inflation ✦

The debate over whether Bitcoin's fixed supply cap should be lifted has resurfaced after StarkWare CEO Eli Ben-Sasson suggested Tuesday that the 21 million cap should be replaced with a 4% annual issuance rate. In a post to X, Ben-Sasson said the current cap "doesn't make sense" because private keys are lost over time and "as time goes to infinity, all keys will be lost." Crypto hardware provider Ledger estimated in November that up to 4 million Bitcoin — nearly 20% of all BTC ever mined — had been burned or permanently lost. Ben-Sasson said he still supports a hard upper bound on Bitcoin's supply and that a 4% annual inflation rate roughly tracks human population growth. The proposal drew sharp criticism from Bitcoiners who argue the fixed cap underpins the "digital gold" narrative and is fundamental to what makes Bitcoin unique. Strategy executive chairman Michael Saylor, who plans to burn his Bitcoin private keys upon his death as a "pro-rata contribution" to other holders, has been one of the strongest advocates of the immutability of Bitcoin's supply cap. Ben-Sasson's comments reignited one of the most contentious debates in the Bitcoin community — the question of whether the 21 million cap is a feature to be preserved at all costs, or a parameter that could be adjusted to address long-term network security incentives.
