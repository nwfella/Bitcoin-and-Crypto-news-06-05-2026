Bitcoin Jumps to $64K on Lowest US CPI Since 2020; Traders Wary of Rejection

Bitcoin surged past $64,000 on Tuesday after the June Consumer Price Index posted its largest monthly decline since April 2020, coming in at 3.5% versus the anticipated 3.8%. The drop, led by a 5.7% decline in energy prices despite headwinds from the Strait of Hormuz crisis, boosted risk assets broadly with US stocks also turning green. Traders welcomed the dovish tilt, with odds of Fed rate hikes dropping sharply, though the CME FedWatch Tool still shows consensus for a 25-basis-point hike at the September meeting. Despite the rally, analysts warned of "lower high" risk with local resistance above $64,000 still firmly in play. Economist Mohamed El-Erian noted the print should "temper what had become an excessively hawkish market tilt."

Humanity Protocol Vows Operational Security Overhaul After $36M Phishing Hack

Humanity Protocol will refocus its cybersecurity efforts on operational security following a June $36 million exploit traced to a compromised employee laptop, founder Terence Kwok told Cointelegraph. The exploit stemmed from last year's mainnet launch, when production keys were inadvertently backed up onto the laptop, including admin hot wallet keys and multisig owner keys. Blockchain security firm Quantstamp said the phishing email pointed to North Korea-linked threat actors. The incident highlights a broader shift as crypto hackers increasingly target staff-level vulnerabilities rather than smart contract code, with phishing driving $508M in Q1 losses and wallet compromises accounting for $807M in Q2, according to CertiK.

✦ Analysis: Trump's Gamble on Graham's Legacy ✦

President Donald Trump's decision to invoke the late Senator Lindsey Graham in pushing the CLARITY Act is a high-stakes legislative maneuver that could reshape US digital asset regulation for a generation. With Graham's death and Senator Mitch McConnell's hospitalization narrowing the Republican majority to 51-47, the bill now requires at least nine Democratic votes to clear the 60-vote threshold — a heavy lift given Democratic demands for stronger conflict-of-interest provisions, particularly around Trump's ties to World Liberty Financial and his memecoin ventures. White House crypto adviser Patrick Witt signaled urgency, saying "[w]e cannot afford to delay any longer," as the Senate faces only four weeks of session before the August recess. The bill would shift digital asset oversight authority from the SEC to the CFTC, a move that has drawn support from crypto industry leaders but skepticism from consumer advocates who warn it could weaken investor protections. Senator Cynthia Lummis backed Trump's call, saying Graham "was passionate about ensuring that American leadership stayed at the forefront of everything — including digital assets."
