Bitcoin Price Falls Up to 4% on Strategy BTC Sale as Trader Sees Summer 2022 Repeat

Bitcoin wiped out its weekend gains and dropped as much as 4% on Monday after Strategy disclosed the sale of 3,588 Bitcoin for $216 million to fund preferred stock dividend payments. The sale reduced Strategy's total holdings to 843,775 BTC, though the firm's $2.55 billion dollar reserve remained untouched. Trader and analyst CryptoAmsterdam warned the move echoes Strategy's 2022 tax-loss selling, after which BTC tumbled 50% over the following months. "The last time Strategy sold Bitcoin, we saw a massive correction," he noted. Bernstein maintained its year-end Bitcoin price target of $150,000 despite the sale, calling the capital framework move "well telegraphed." The sale included 1,363 BTC at an average of $59,256 and 2,225 BTC at $60,773.

Thousands of Crypto Wallets at Risk from 'Ill Bloom' Vulnerability: Coinspect

Blockchain security research firm Coinspect has disclosed a vulnerability dubbed "Ill Bloom" affecting thousands of crypto wallets across Bitcoin, Ethereum, Polygon, Rootstock, Tron and Solana. The exploit stems from weak randomness — an insecure pseudorandom number generator — used during recovery phrase generation on certain software wallets, particularly lesser-known mobile wallets. At least $5 million has been drained from exposed wallets since May 27, though the true figure may be significantly higher. "If funds recently moved without your permission, this vulnerability may be why," Coinspect warned in its disclosure. Wallets generated as early as 2018 are at risk, and Coinspect urged users to migrate to hardware wallets or established software wallets with audited entropy sources.

Belgian Regulator Flags 6 Unauthorized Crypto Providers After MiCA Deadline

Belgium's Financial Services and Markets Authority (FSMA) has warned consumers against six crypto-asset service providers operating without authorization, days after the EU's MiCA transitional period expired on July 1. The regulator named Aurum Foundation, Bank Bit, Bithf Pro, Dxago, Global Dynamic Trade and ZeriaFunding, adding them to its list of fraudulent CASPs. The warning signals that national regulators across Europe are beginning active enforcement under MiCA, which entered into force at the end of 2024. Under FSMA guidance, only authorized CASPs may offer custody, trading platforms, crypto-to-fiat exchange, order execution and portfolio management services. Binance withdrew its MiCA application in Greece just before the deadline, seeking authorization in another EU jurisdiction instead.

FCA Warns of Major Shakeup as AI Agents Meet Tokenized Money

The UK's Financial Conduct Authority has issued a landmark 147-page report warning that retail financial services are hurtling toward total automation driven by autonomous "agentic AI." ✦ UK FCA's 'Mills Report' Positions Stablecoins as Critical Infrastructure for Agentic AI Finance ✦

The FCA's landmark 147-page "Mills Report" marks a pivotal moment for the intersection of AI and crypto, explicitly positioning systemic stablecoins and tokenized bank deposits as potential settlement infrastructure for autonomous AI-driven financial services. The report argues that traditional multi-day settlement latency remains an operational bottleneck for machine-speed finance, and that programmable money living natively on ledger networks provides the friction-free, atomic settlement needed for automated protocols to move capital instantly without human clearance. At the far end of the "autonomy spectrum" the FCA envisions, humans act as mere "observers" while AI continuously manages capital. The report recommends "enabling the foundations for agentic finance" through trusted agent protocols, scaling up the FCA's AI Lab, and developing regulatory frameworks that can accommodate autonomous financial agents operating at machine speed. With 20% of UK adults already open to letting AI make autonomous financial choices, and more than 20 frontier AI models released since late 2025 alone, the FCA warns that the acceleration has outpaced prior regulatory timelines. The report notes that firms are "moving from systems that recommend actions to systems empowered and trained to take them," positioning 2026 as the year agentic AI finance moves from theoretical to operational.
