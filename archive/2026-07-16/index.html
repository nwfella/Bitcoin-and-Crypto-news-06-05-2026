US Senate Unanimously Adopts Resolution Opposing Clemency for SBF

The US Senate has adopted a resolution opposing executive clemency for former FTX CEO Sam Bankman-Fried, the convicted crypto executive behind one of the industry's largest collapses. The Senate agreed by unanimous consent to S. Res. 772 — a nonbinding measure introduced on June 17 by Senator Ruben Gallego with Senator Cynthia Lummis as cosponsor — stating that Bankman-Fried should not receive executive clemency. The resolution affirms the Senate's commitment to the rule of law and the integrity of the US financial system following Bankman-Fried's conviction on fraud and conspiracy charges. While the measure cannot block a presidential pardon, it reflects strong bipartisan opposition after Bankman-Fried sought clemency from President Donald Trump in June. Prediction markets on Polymarket now put the odds of a Trump pardon by July 31 below 1%. Senator Bernie Moreno of Ohio joined as a cosponsor on Tuesday, adding Republican support to the bipartisan measure.

Trump to Meet Senators Over CLARITY Act on Thursday as August Recess Looms

US President Donald Trump is set to meet with several senators at the White House on Thursday to discuss progress on the crypto market structure bill. According to Politico, Senator Bernie Moreno said a group of senators will brief the president on the bill and "its path to success." Senator Cynthia Lummis will also attend. "We'll be talking about the entirety of the bill. I mean, obviously the president's been very engaged in this bill," said Moreno. "He's the one who's really driven the innovation that I think will pay dividends." The meeting comes as lawmakers race to pass the CLARITY Act before the Senate's August recess — seen as the last realistic opportunity to pass legislation before the midterm elections. Senator Thom Tillis, who has been working through unresolved provisions, told Politico: "I think it's critical if we're going to try and get this across the floor before August recess."

Bitcoin Hits $65.5K as PPI Delivers Second Day of Surprise Inflation Data

Bitcoin surged to $65,500 on Wednesday — its highest since June 22 — after the June Producer Price Index came in cooler than expected at 5.5% year-on-year, following a 0.3% monthly decline, per Bureau of Labor Statistics data. The print follows Tuesday's downside CPI surprise, which had already boosted risk assets despite macro pressure from the US-Iran conflict. Economist Mohamed El-Erian called the figures "much better-than-expected," saying they are "set to boost equities and further temper market expectations for upcoming interest rate hikes." The CME FedWatch Tool now shows a 0.25% rate hike in September is no longer the most likely scenario, marking a significant shift in market expectations. However, traders remained cautious about Bitcoin's ability to sustain gains above $65,000, noting bear-market history and local resistance levels.

US AG Nominee Todd Blanche Faces Grilling Over Crypto Enforcement and CZ Pardon

President Donald Trump's nominee for US Attorney General, Todd Blanche, faced sharp questioning from both sides of the aisle during his Senate Judiciary Committee confirmation hearing. Senator Dick Durbin accused Blanche of dismantling the Justice Department's crypto enforcement unit and ending active investigations into the digital asset sector, arguing the shift benefited Trump's crypto interests including World Liberty Financial. Republican Senator Thom Tillis raised concerns about the presidential pardon of former Binance CEO Changpeng Zhao, who pleaded guilty to Anti-Money Laundering violations in 2023. Blanche rejected no allegations directly but said he would review the pardon process if confirmed. The hearing underscores the deepening politicization of crypto enforcement in Washington as the CLARITY Act heads toward a Senate vote.

Tokenized Stocks Market Cap Reaches Record $2.3B, Led by Ondo and Kraken

The global market capitalization of tokenized stocks rose to a record $2.3 billion on Wednesday, as more investors sought exposure to blockchain-based equity products. Ethereum boasted the largest market share at 34%, followed by BNB Chain with 30% and Solana with 23%, according to Token Terminal data. The largest increase came from Kraken's xStocks, accounting for $507 million in tokenized equities, and Binance's bStocks with $334 million. Ondo Finance remained the largest tokenized stock issuer with $955 million in onchain equities. Kraken launched access to 11,000 US-listed stocks and ETFs through xStocks in April 2025, and cumulative trading volume has exceeded $25 billion within about eight months. Binance opened zero-commission trading to over 7,000 US tokenized stocks on June 1 as part of its strategy to become a multi-asset platform.

✦ Analysis: Visa & Artemis — The AI Agent Economy Faces Payment Infrastructure Gaps ✦

Artificial intelligence agents are challenging incumbent global card payment infrastructure that struggles to process high-frequency micropayments, according to a joint report from payments giant Visa and investment thesis platform Artemis. The report, published Wednesday, found that traditional cards were built for human commerce with low-frequency transactions — insufficient for AI agents that need near-zero fees and faster settlement to make agentic micropayments commercially viable. New infrastructure is increasingly necessary since AI agents crossed a key capability threshold in mid-2025, enabling them to discover unfamiliar APIs, evaluate prices, and decide on autonomous payments. Coinbase's x402 payment protocol has already processed $15 million in adjusted volume across over 109 million transactions since May 2025, with monthly transactions exploding from 40,000 to 3.8 million in October alone. The report found that a single machine-payment framework could support both stablecoin and traditional card transactions, concluding: "The trajectory points toward convergence rather than competition — cards for proxy purchases inside existing merchant networks, stablecoins for machine-native micropayments, and hybrid flows where both are used within the same workflow." Australian exchange Swyftx estimated that AI-enabled microbusinesses could drive an additional $262 billion in stablecoin volume by 2033.
