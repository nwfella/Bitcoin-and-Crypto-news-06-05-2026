Sony Bank Gets US Regulator Nod to Issue Stablecoins

Sony Bank received preliminary approval from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) to establish Connectia Trust, a US national trust bank subsidiary that will issue dollar-denominated stablecoins. Revolut Says USDT Delisting Limited to EEA and Switzerland

Revolut clarified that its USDT delisting will only affect customers in the European Economic Area and Switzerland, while support for the stablecoin continues in other markets globally. The decision followed a review under MiCA regulation, reflecting a broader trend across the EU where crypto platforms are phasing out USDT after Tether chose not to seek MiCA authorization. Revolut had already removed USDT from its Revolut X trading platform for EEA customers. The company said the delisting will be complete by August 31, 2026. Switzerland, notably, is not part of the EU or EEA and is not directly covered by MiCA, though Revolut included it among affected markets.

✦ Swift Launches Blockchain Ledger With 17-Bank Tokenized Deposit Pilot ✦

The world's largest financial messaging network, Swift, announced that its blockchain-based ledger is ready for initial use after nine months of development. Seventeen major banks — including HSBC, Citi, BNP Paribas, UBS, ANZ, DBS and Standard Chartered — are preparing to pilot cross-border payments using tokenized bank deposits on the new ledger. The ledger will enable participating banks to support 24/7 cross-border payments, including overnight and weekend transactions, while maintaining existing compliance, credit, risk and control standards. Swift said it plans to expand the ledger's functionality after the initial controlled go-live phase. The interbank network connects over 11,500 financial institutions across more than 200 countries, with 75% of payments on its existing network already reaching beneficiary banks within 10 minutes. This marks a significant step in banks' efforts to use tokenized deposits within regulated financial infrastructure while extending payment availability beyond traditional banking hours.
