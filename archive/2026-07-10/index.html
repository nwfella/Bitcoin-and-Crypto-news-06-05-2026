USDC Issuer Circle Wins Final OCC Approval for National Trust Bank

Circle received final approval from the US Office of the Comptroller of the Currency to establish First National Digital Currency Bank, a national trust bank that will operate as Circle National Trust. CEO Jeremy Allaire hailed the move as "a defining step in bringing blockchain technology and digital assets into the core of the US financial system." The bank will initially provide fiduciary digital asset custody services for Circle and its affiliates, with potential future expansion to institutional clients. Circle's stock surged approximately 16% in pre-market trading following the announcement. USDC, the second-largest stablecoin at $73.3 billion market cap, has grown 16.7% over the past year.

✦ Japan's Bitcoin-Backed Lending Revolution Takes Shape ✦

Japanese lender CRYL has launched Bitcoin-backed loans of up to 1 billion yen ($6.2 million), allowing individuals and businesses to raise fiat currency without selling their BTC. Borrowers can access between $6,200 and $6.2 million at annual rates of 3.5% to 7% with collateral ratios of 40% to 60%. The one-year loans can be used for expenses including taxes, business funding and property purchases. The launch expands Japan's small but growing market for regulated crypto-backed financing, following Fintertech's earlier entry which lends up to $3 million against Bitcoin or Ether. Meanwhile, Metaplanet Securities, yen stablecoin issuer JPYC, and tokenization provider Progmat have announced a joint study into using BTC as collateral for digital corporate bonds. Together, these developments signal that Bitcoin is steadily being integrated into the fabric of Japanese credit markets, offering holders a third option beyond simply holding or selling their digital assets.
