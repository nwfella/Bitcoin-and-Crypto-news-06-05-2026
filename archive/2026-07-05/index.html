Bitcoin reached its highest levels in nearly a fortnight over the weekend, with BTC/USD climbing to $63,450 on Saturday amid thinner exchange order books and a three-day US holiday weekend. Vitalik Buterin Unveils 'Lean Ethereum' Roadmap — Quantum, Scalability, Privacy

Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin has named quantum resistance, scalability and privacy as three of Ethereum's top priorities under a new "Lean Ethereum" strawmap, laying out the network's technical direction for the remainder of the decade. In a post to X on Saturday, Buterin said the collection of upgrades will roll out over the next three to four years, touching nearly every layer of Ethereum in a transformation he compared in scale to the September 2022 Merge. "Quantum safety has shifted up a LOT in priority," he said, adding that finalizing a quantum-safe solution for blobs has "become urgent." Enhancing privacy is another priority, now a "first class goal." The roadmap shift comes amid a series of changes at the Ethereum Foundation, which laid off roughly 20% of its staff last month.

Bitcoin Realized P&L Ratio Falls to 43-Month Low — Historically a Bottom Signal

Bitcoin's realized profit and loss ratio has fallen to a 43-month low of -0.35, a figure that signals extreme market-wide loss conditions but has historically coincided with market bottoms, according to CryptoQuant. The metric hasn't fallen this low since December 2022, shortly after FTX's collapse. "Historically the indicator has marked BTC bottoms with extreme precision," CryptoQuant said. In 2015 and 2019, the ratio fell below -0.35 before price rallies followed. Bitwise CIO Matt Hougan said the bottom is "closer than ever," while Swan Bitcoin analyst Adam Livingston recommended buying now at a discount rather than overpaying later. The data adds weight to the argument that capitulation may be nearing its end after weeks of persistent selling pressure.

Revolut to Delist USDT in August, Citing Regulatory and Risk Concerns

Revolut, the crypto-friendly digital banking platform, notified some users it will delist Tether's USDT stablecoin in August, citing regulatory and risk concerns. Users will no longer be able to buy USDT starting July 6, with full delisting scheduled for Aug. 31, 2026. If users do not sell or withdraw their USDT by end of August, Revolut will automatically convert remaining holdings into users' base currency. The move follows similar delistings by Coinbase in Europe in 2024 to align with MiCA requirements. Tether's CEO has repeatedly criticized MiCA's reserve requirements as "not well thought" legislation. The delisting signals growing regulatory pressure on stablecoins in European markets.

South Africa Proposes Crypto Tax Guidance Under Existing Framework

South Africa's tax authority, SARS, has proposed draft guidelines clarifying how crypto assets are taxed under existing income and capital gains tax rules. The draft provides that most crypto activities — trading, swapping and spending — are generally treated as disposals that may trigger tax events. South Africa is one of Africa's largest crypto markets, with at least 5.8 million residents holding crypto assets. Public input on the draft is open until August 31.
