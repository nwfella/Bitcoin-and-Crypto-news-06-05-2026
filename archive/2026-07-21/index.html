Regulation UK

UK Parliamentary Group Probes Banking Barriers for Crypto Firms

A United Kingdom parliamentary group has launched an inquiry into whether crypto businesses and consumers face unfair barriers to banking services, including account access and restrictions on crypto-related transactions. The Crypto and Digital Assets All-Party Parliamentary Group (APPG) said it would examine how the restrictions affect investment, competition and economic growth, and assess whether they are proportionate. Written submissions from banks, payment providers, crypto firms and other stakeholders are open until Aug. 31, after which the group plans to publish its findings and recommendations. A January survey by the UK Cryptoasset Business Council found that 10 crypto exchanges reported banks blocked or delayed 40% of transactions to crypto platforms, and 70% of respondents said the restrictions had reduced their willingness to invest, expand or hire in the UK.

Regulation Russia

Russian Parliament Passes Law Regulating Crypto Market

Russia's State Duma has approved bill No. 1194918-8, titled "On Digital Currency and Digital Rights," in its second and third readings, moving the country closer to its first comprehensive crypto market framework. If enacted, the bill would establish rules for crypto market participants including exchanges, brokers, asset managers and custodians, while allowing crypto assets to be used in foreign trade operations. The Bank of Russia would gain broad oversight authority to determine which crypto assets may be offered through licensed intermediaries. Non-qualified investors would face a 300,000-ruble (about $3,800) annual cap on crypto purchases. The bill still requires approval by the Federation Council and President Vladimir Putin's signature, with most provisions taking effect Sept. 1, 2026.

Adoption Telegram

Pavel Durov Says Telegram to Roll Out Native Gram Crypto Wallet

Telegram founder Pavel Durov announced the messaging platform will roll out a native non-custodial Gram wallet this summer, bringing self-custody crypto transactions to its more than 1 billion monthly active users. Durov said the wallet would allow users to send cryptocurrency instantly and without fees, describing it as the "largest rollout of a non-custodial crypto wallet in human history." The announcement comes weeks after The Open Network renamed its native token from Toncoin to Gram, reviving the cryptocurrency's original name from Telegram's 2018 white paper. Telegram has increasingly backed the TON ecosystem since abandoning its original blockchain project following a 2020 SEC settlement.

Institutions ETFs

Grayscale Files S-1 for First US Worldcoin ETF

Crypto asset manager Grayscale on Monday filed an S-1 registration statement for the first US Worldcoin ETF, which would give investors exposure to the WLD token through an exchange-traded product. According to the preliminary prospectus, the Grayscale Worldcoin ETF is intended to list on the Nasdaq under the symbol GWLD. BitGo Bank & Trust would custody the Worldcoin, while BNY Mellon would serve as administrator and transfer agent. WLD is an ERC-20 token built on Ethereum that serves as the native token of World, a project using biometric verification to distinguish humans from bots, founded by OpenAI CEO Sam Altman. The proposed ETF adds to 17 crypto-related exchange-traded products offered by Grayscale, including those tracking BTC, XRP, SOL, ETH, DOGE and LINK.
