Bitcoin Threatens $62K in Risk-Asset Rout as Trump Says US Will 'Run' Closed Hormuz Strait

Bitcoin edged closer to $62,000 on Monday as US stocks opened lower amid escalating rhetoric between the United States and Iran over the Strait of Hormuz. Regulation South America

Bolivia Mulls Recognizing USDT as Payment Currency Amid Dollar Shortage

Bolivia is evaluating a regulatory framework to allow Tether's USDT stablecoin to circulate alongside the boliviano and US dollar as it seeks to ease a persistent dollar shortage. Economy and Public Finance Minister Jose Gabriel Espinoza said the proposal would treat USDT as "just another currency," enabling its use for payments, savings and trade if approved. Authorities are reviewing anti-money laundering safeguards as Bolivia remains on the FATF grey list. The plan builds on Bolivia's shift toward digital assets following the lifting of its cryptocurrency ban in 2024. President Rodrigo Paz Pereira's administration has pledged to integrate crypto into the formal financial system, including allowing banks to offer stablecoin-related products. The push comes as Bolivia faces a prolonged US dollar shortage following pressure on foreign exchange reserves and the collapse of its long-standing currency peg.

Corporate Strategy

Strategy Sells $467M in MSTR Shares, Leaves 843,775 BTC Stack Untouched

Strategy, the largest corporate holder of Bitcoin, raised $466.7 million by selling 4.8 million MSTR shares through its at-the-market offering between July 6 and July 12, according to an SEC filing. The company did not buy or sell any Bitcoin during the period and reported holdings of 843,775 BTC at an average purchase price of $75,476 per BTC. Strategy's US dollar reserve grew to $3 billion as of July 12, up from $2.55 billion a week earlier, used to fund dividend payments on its preferred stock and interest on outstanding debt. The company has $23.8 billion of remaining capacity under its MSTR ATM offering. MSTR shares were trading down roughly 3% to $91.80 ahead of Monday's Nasdaq open.

Markets ETFs

Bitcoin ETFs Draw $197M, Snap 8-Week Outflow Streak

US-listed spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds recorded a net inflow of $197.4 million in the week ended Friday, snapping an eight-week streak of weekly outflows dating back to May. Most of the week's gains came from the BlackRock iShares Bitcoin Trust ETF, which recorded $291.9 million in inflows, offset by outflows from the Grayscale Bitcoin Trust ETF, Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund and ARK 21 Shares Bitcoin ETF. Monochrome Asset Management founder Jeff Yew said the inflows come "at a time when institutional confidence is growing around the potential passage of the CLARITY Act." However, 10x Research founder Markus Thielen cautioned that ETF and stablecoin outflows and seasonality in August and September remain headwinds. The weekly inflow was modest compared with the $8.26 billion investors withdrew since May 11.

Hyundai Completes USDT Treasury Settlement Pilot Between US and Mexico

Hyundai has completed a USDT treasury settlement pilot between the United States and Mexico, marking a significant milestone for stablecoin adoption in corporate cross-border payments. The pilot, which utilized Tether's USDT stablecoin, demonstrates how dollar-pegged digital assets can streamline international treasury operations by reducing settlement times and costs associated with traditional correspondent banking. The successful trial comes as stablecoin adoption accelerates among multinational corporations seeking more efficient payment rails. The development follows a broader trend of corporate adoption of stablecoins for real-world treasury management, with USDT's market capitalization now exceeding $184 billion.

Japan's SBI to Launch Yen Stablecoin Lending With 3% Yield

Japan's SBI Holdings announced plans to launch a yen stablecoin lending service offering 3% yield, marking one of the first regulated stablecoin yield products in the country. The service would allow users to earn returns on their yen-pegged digital asset holdings through SBI's regulated platform. The move follows Japan's proactive regulatory approach to stablecoins, which were formally recognized under the country's payment laws in 2023. SBI's initiative signals growing institutional appetite for stablecoin-based financial products in Asia's second-largest economy.
