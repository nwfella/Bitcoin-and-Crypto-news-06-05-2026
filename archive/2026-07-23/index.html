BitMEX Begins Wind-Down Process After 11-Year Run in Crypto Derivatives

BitMEX, one of the pioneers of cryptocurrency derivatives trading, announced it will shut down operations on Sept. 23, 2026, after owner and operator HDR Global Trading Limited decided to close the company following a strategic review. The exchange has stopped accepting new account registrations immediately but will continue operating normally until the closure date. Risk limits will be introduced on Aug. 26, preventing users from opening new positions while allowing them to reduce existing exposure. The BMEX token plunged approximately 90% following the announcement, and the exchange urged users to close positions and withdraw funds before the deadline. BitMEX said its proof-of-reserves process shows that user assets exceed liabilities, and users who do not withdraw before the deadline will still be able to access wallet balances after trading services cease.

Hackers Steal $31.6M in Two Crypto Bridge Exploits Within 7 Hours

Hackers stole more than $31.6 million across two unrelated crypto bridge exploits spaced just hours apart, targeting bridges operated by decentralized perpetual exchange AFX and Verus Protocol. According to Blockaid, AFX on Arbitrum lost $24.15 million on Wednesday through a hack targeting one of its cross-chain bridges. Hours later, an exploit targeting the Verus Ethereum Bridge resulted in about $7.5 million stolen. Blockaid CEO Ido Ben-Natan said the AFX incident appeared to be an operational security incident rather than a smart contract vulnerability, with five hot validator keys compromised. "Bridges will always be a weak link, until security is upgraded," onchain investigator TheCrypticWolf said on X.

HYPE Falls as Crypto Funds Queue Nearly $150M in Locked Tokens for Withdrawal

Hyperliquid's HYPE token briefly fell 8% to around $58 on Wednesday as Multicoin Capital, Selini Capital and Galaxy Digital queued nearly $150 million of the token for unstaking. Multicoin Capital alone holds a combined $138.78 million in staked HYPE, of which roughly 83% — about $116 million — sits in pending withdrawal. The withdrawal queue nearly doubles the daily spot turnover of roughly $72.8 million, helping explain recent market apprehension. HYPE has fallen about 11% over the past week. However, whether the unlocked tokens are about to be sold remains unclear — Selini's unstaking appears linked to the shutdown of HIP-3 CASH perpetuals markets, and the funds may sell through OTC desks.

Goldman Sachs CEO Backs 'Not Perfect' CLARITY Act as Senate Vote Expected

David Solomon, chair and CEO of Goldman Sachs, has given his support for the cryptocurrency market structure bill under consideration in the US Senate, even as many provisions continue to divide lawmakers. According to a Thursday Politico report, Solomon said the Digital Asset Market Clarity (CLARITY) Act was "not perfect" but needed to create a "level playing field to enhance market stability." Solomon stands out as the head of a major financial company backing the legislation, which many of his peers are opposing by arguing that it allows crypto companies to pay interest on stablecoins outside rules for financial institutions. Republican lawmakers released the text of the CLARITY Act on Wednesday ahead of a potential vote in the Senate, including ethics provisions that have concerned many Democrats over President Trump's crypto investments.

Strategy-Led Group Pledges $15M to Quantum-Proof the Bitcoin Network

Michael Saylor's Strategy announced the launch of the Bitcoin Security Consortium, a group of financial institutions and Bitcoin companies supporting the long-term quantum security of the Bitcoin network. The consortium pledged an aggregate $15 million over three years to support developers securing the network against quantum computing threats. Other founding members include Anchorage Digital, ARK Invest, BlackRock, Block, Blockstream, Coinbase, Fidelity Digital Assets and Galaxy. BlackRock's global head of digital assets, Robert Mitchnick, said Bitcoin core developers do "incredibly important work" and the firm was pleased to support the network's long-term security. Galaxy Digital separately pledged up to $5 million in grants for quantum-security developers.

US Officials Barred Until 2029 From Issuing Tokens Under CLARITY Ethics Rules

Senate Republicans have released the proposed 616-page text for the CLARITY Act, including language that would bar all US federal officials — including President Donald Trump — from issuing or sponsoring any digital asset. The White House described the ethics provisions as the "most comprehensive and wide-ranging ethics provision in history." The ban would be temporary, expiring on Jan. 20, 2029 — the day Trump's second term ends. The US Attorney General would be responsible for enforcing the ban. The bill still needs support from several Democratic lawmakers to meet a 60-vote threshold, and many Democrats have said they will not vote without strong ethics language to address the president's "crypto corruption."
